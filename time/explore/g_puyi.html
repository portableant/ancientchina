Last Emperor Puyi gains throne

In 1908, the last Qing emperor, Puyi, became Emperor at the age of the three. He was only to rule for three years. In 1911 revolutionaries formed a Republican government and forced him to stand down.

His abdication brought the two thousand year old Chinese empire to an end.

In 1911 the revolutionaries declared a Provisional Republican government. However, the revolutionaries were still weak. For the next twenty years China was virtually ruled by a number of warlords. No single power was strong enough to take control. This meant that Japan was able to easily invade China in 1931.
